Princess Diana, born Diana Frances Spencer on July 1, 1961, was the first wife of Charles, Prince of Wales, and the mother of Princes William and Harry. Known for her compassionate nature and dedication to charitable work, Diana was deeply involved in various causes, including those related to HIV/AIDS awareness, landmine clearance, and children's hospitals. Her marriage to Prince Charles, marked by both high-profile moments and personal struggles, captivated the world, but they eventually divorced in 1996. Diana's tragic death in a car crash in Paris on August 31, 1997, shocked the globe and left a lasting legacy of kindness, philanthropy, and a commitment to humanitarian efforts.
